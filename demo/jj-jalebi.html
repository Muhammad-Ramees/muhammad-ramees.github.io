The Story

Since 1947 — The Legend Behind the Jalebi

✦

Born in the chaos and hope of Partition-era Bombay. Named by the city itself. Trusted for 75 years.

It was 1947 — the year India was reborn. On the bustling streets of Mohammed Ali Road, Nagpada, a young man named Rafeek Shaikh began making jalebis the old way. No shortcuts. No frozen dough. No compromises.

The name "JJ Jalebi" wasn't chosen by Rafeek — it was given by Mumbai itself. Customers from JJ Hospital nearby would cross the road and come back every morning. "Let's go to the JJ wala," they'd say. The name stuck.

Seventy-five years later, the same secret recipe still survives. The same multi-grain flour. The same herbs, still sourced directly from Bombay. The same dark, cream-filled mawa jalebi that has no equal in any city.
